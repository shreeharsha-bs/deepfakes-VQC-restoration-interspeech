Layered Generative Intervention in Deepfake Detection

Decomposing speech authenticity into source identity and processing mediation

Speech authenticity is compositional, not binary. Modern deepfake detectors operate on degrees of algorithmic mediation rather than on an intrinsic notion of realness.
Overview Dataset Confusion Matrices

Overview

We challenge the binary “real vs. fake” framing of deepfake speech detection. Speech can undergo multiple layers of generative processing — TTS synthesis, voice conversion, voice quality modification — and each layer adds algorithmic mediation. We define four intervention depths and show that what current detectors actually learn is processing depth, not source identity.

Four Intervention Depths

ClassLabelDescription
Real0Original human recordings — no processing
Real→Conv1Human audio processed through voice conversion
Fake2Raw TTS output — synthesis only
Fake→Conv3TTS output + voice conversion — two layers of processing

MLP 4-Way Classifier (SSL Embeddings)

Trained on concatenated HuBERT + Whisper + Wav2Vec2 XLS-R 1B embeddings (2816-dim). Evaluated on seen architectures, unseen OuteTTS, and cross-domain ASVspoof5.

DF-Arena 4-Way (MLAAD-only Fine-Tuning)

DF-Arena 1B fine-tuned from binary to 4-way classification on MLAAD. On ASVspoof5, the model classifies nearly everything as Fake or Fake→Conv.

DF-Arena 4-Way — Training Data Progression on ASVspoof5

Adding ASVspoof5 to the training mix recovers real speech recognition (0.1% → 94.7%) and reduces source EER from 46.3% to 11.8%. Adding Sidon further stabilizes generalization.
